Skewness in Histograms

The skew is named after the tail — not the hump.

Negative Skew
Left-skewed
←
Tail points LEFT
Mean < Median
relationship
Most values bunch near the top, but a few low outliers drag the tail to the left.
e.g. Age at death in a developed country
No Skew
Symmetric / Normal
←
Equal both sides
→
Mean = Median
relationship
Data is balanced around the centre. Tails are equal on both sides.
e.g. Heights of students in a class
Positive Skew
Right-skewed
Tail points RIGHT
→
Mean > Median
relationship
Most values bunch near the low end, but a few large outliers drag the tail to the right.
e.g. Income — most earn modest salaries, a few earn millions
💡

The Golden Rule

The name of the skew tells you which direction the tail points — not where the hump is. Positive = tail right. Negative = tail left. The mean always gets pulled in the direction of the tail, so it ends up on the same side as the skew name.
